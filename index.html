For workers · what makes this different

  • You own your work. Every annotation is signed with YOUR key. You export your history. You take it to another platform when you want. No lock-in.
  • Multi-rail payment. Stripe stuck? Use PayPal. PayPal frozen? Use Wise. All blocked? Use crypto. Invoice instead. Your money flows.
  • Peer review · not algorithm. When your work is challenged, real humans grade · with signed votes. Appeals work. Algorithms don't accidentally ban you.
  • Global by default. If you can ID-verify and the rail can reach you, you're in. No US-favoritism. No "this country isn't supported."
  • Higher rates per item. Cutting the middleman by 30-40% means the worker takes home more, the LLM client pays the same. Win-win.

For LLM clients · what makes this different

  • Signed provenance. Every item in your training set ships with cryptographic proof of who annotated it, when, after how many revisions. Audit-grade.
  • No vendor lock. The platform is MIT. You can verify the pipeline. Self-host if you want. Migrate workers in/out.
  • Sovereign data pipeline. Your data doesn't sit on a centralized server you don't control. Use fallcdn / fallcore / fallcompass · run the rating layer wherever you trust.
  • Better worker economics = better worker quality = better data. We're not racing to the bottom.
